  "details": "This document is drafted and sent by the Plaintiff's attorney (law firm) representing an injured party. The recipient is the claims adjuster or representative at an insurance company (either the at-fault party's liability insurer or the plaintiff's own Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist carrier). The primary purpose of the document is to formally demand a monetary settlement (often the maximum policy limits) to resolve a personal injury claim without going to trial. It strategically outlines liability and heavily details the plaintiff's medical damages to financially justify the demand. It typically includes a strict time-sensitive deadline (e.g., 7 to 30 days) and includes 'bad faith' language, warning the insurance company of legal consequences if they fail to protect their insured from excess judgments by rejecting a reasonable settlement offer."
