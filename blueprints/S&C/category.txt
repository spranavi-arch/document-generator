It is filed with the Court (e.g., Supreme Court of the State of New York) to officially initiate a civil lawsuit, specifically for personal injury/negligence. The primary recipient is the Defendant, who must be formally served with the document. The document notifies the Defendant of the legal claims against them and mandates a response (Answer or Notice of Appearance) within a strict timeframe (20 or 30 days) to avoid a default judgment. Key details required to generate this document include: Plaintiff and Defendant names and addresses, specific court venue, date and location of the accident, vehicle specifics (make, year, license plate), description of the incident (e.g., striking an e-scooter or parked car), specific statutory references (like CPLR 1601/1602 and NY Insurance Law §5102(d)), and the justification for the attorney verifying the complaint instead of the plaintiff (e.g., plaintiff is not in the county where the attorney's office is located)."
